Mental Disorders

Depression

Depression is a very common mental illness in the US. It is different from feelings of sadness or grief because depression can last for months and/or years. However, grief such as the death of loved ones or a long term relationship is ended can lead to depression. https://www.nami.org/Learn-More/Mental-Health-Conditions/Depression

Anxiety Disorder

Anxiety Disorder is a mental disorder defined by feelings of extreme worry, fear, and uneasiness. It differs from normal anxiety because everyone feels anxious and stressed to some degree. Anxiety becomes a disorder when it starts to interfere with daily activities. https://www.nami.org/Learn-More/Mental-Health-Conditions/Anxiety-Disorder

Schizophrenia

Schizophrenia is a serious mental health illness which causes delusions, hallucinations, bizarre behaviors, feeling of being watched. According to Mental Health America, people who were diagnosed with Schizophrenia usually cannot distinguish the difference between reality or imagination. https://www.nami.org/Learn-More/Mental-Health-Conditions/Schizophrenia

Eating Disorder

Eating Disorder is an illness characterized by abnormal eating behaviors that affect a person’s physical and mental health negatively. The two popular eating disorders are anorexia and bulimia. People with these two eating disorders usually desire to be thin, perfect and have an excessive fear of obesity. Not only do women suffer from eating disorder, but also men. https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics/eating-disorders/index.shtml

OCD

People who diagnosed with Obsessive Compulsive Disorder are constantly afraid of germs, feel the need to do things repeatedly and arrange things in a specific way. This disorder can drive people to obsessively spend hours performing certain routines. https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/ocd/what-is-obsessive-compulsive-disorder

PTSD

PTSD is a short term for Post Traumatic Stress Disorder. A person can develop the disorder after witnessing or being exposed to scary, traumatic, or life-threatening experiences. Some common symptoms for PTSD are flashbacks or nightmares of the situations that bring back trauma . PTSD United stated that approximately 8% Americans can have PTSD at any given time.

Autism

Autism is a mental disorder characterized by impaired communication abilities and social interactions. Statistics have shown that an estimated of 1 in 68 children has been diagnosed with autism. A person with autism may have trouble understanding words, talking to others, and be extremely uncomfortable with physical touch.

ADHD

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is the most common mental health disorder diagnosed in childhood. According to CDC, about 11% of children aged 4-17 have been identified with ADHD. However, boys are mo re likely to develop ADHD than girls. Children with ADHD have trouble paying attention, are impatient and hyperactive.

SUD

Substance Use Disorder is a serious disorder that affects people mentally and physically. SUD is defined as a disorder characterized excessive alcohol and drugs consumption. The signs of substance abuse are physical and psychological dependences on the substance. Exposure to stress, violence, child abuse, or traumatic event can lead to substance abuse.

Dementia

Dementia is a decline in memory and mental disability. It is a severe syndrome that can interfere with daily life. People often confuse dementia and Alzheimer’s. The Alzheimer’s disease is the most common type of dementia. Some common signs of dementia are difficulty communicating with others, memory loss, confusion, and struggling to follow directions.
